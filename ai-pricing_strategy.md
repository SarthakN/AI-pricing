# AI Product Pricing Models

AI products use a mix of traditional SaaS pricing and newer usage/value-based models. The right model usually depends on **how directly the product creates measurable value** and **how predictable usage is**.

---

## 1. Usage-Based Pricing (Most Common in AI)
**You pay for what you consume**

### Examples:
- Tokens (LLMs like APIs)
- API calls
- Images generated
- Minutes of audio processed

### Why it works:
- AI costs scale with compute → aligns pricing with cost
- Flexible for developers

### When to use:
- API products
- Infrastructure / developer tools

### PM Insight:
This is the **default for AI infra**, but can feel unpredictable to buyers → often paired with caps or credits.

---

## 2. Subscription (SaaS-style)
**Flat monthly or annual fee**

### Examples:
- ChatGPT-style plans
- AI writing tools (Jasper, Copy.ai)

### Why it works:
- Predictable revenue
- Easy for users to understand

### When to use:
- End-user tools
- Productivity apps

### Variants:
- Tiered plans (Basic / Pro / Enterprise)
- Feature gating (advanced models, speed, limits)

---

## 3. Hybrid (Subscription + Usage)
**Base fee + variable usage**

### Example:
- $50/month + $0.01 per request

### Why it works:
- Covers baseline cost
- Captures heavy users

### When to use:
- B2B SaaS with AI features
- Platforms with variable intensity users

> 👉 This is becoming the **dominant model in AI SaaS**

---

## 4. Credit-Based Pricing
**Users buy credits, spend across features**

### Examples:
- “1000 credits = X generations”
- Different actions consume different credits

### Why it works:
- Abstracts complexity of tokens/compute
- Gives flexibility across features

### When to use:
- Multi-feature AI tools (text + image + video)

### PM Insight:
Credits = **UX wrapper over usage pricing**

---

## 5. Per-Seat Pricing (with AI limits)
**Pay per user, with usage caps**

### Examples:
- $30/user/month with fair usage
- Enterprise copilots

### Why it works:
- Familiar enterprise model
- Easier budgeting

### When to use:
- Workplace tools (AI copilots, CRM AI, ATS AI)

### Risk:
Heavy users can blow up costs → needs throttling

---

## 6. Outcome-Based / Value-Based Pricing (Most Powerful, Hardest)
**Price based on business impact**

### Examples:
- % of revenue generated
- Cost per hire (for recruitment AI)
- Cost per qualified lead

### Why it works:
- Aligns price with value
- Can capture massive upside

### When to use:
- Clear ROI products
- Automation replacing human labor

### PM Insight:
This is where AI pricing is heading long-term

---

## 7. Freemium + Paywall
**Free tier to drive adoption**

### Examples:
- Limited generations per day
- Watermarked outputs

### Why it works:
- AI is experiential → users need to try it

### When to use:
- Product-led growth (PLG) strategy

---

## 8. Enterprise Custom Pricing
**Negotiated deals**

### Includes:
- Volume discounts
- SLA pricing
- Dedicated infrastructure

### When to use:
- Large customers
- High-volume use cases

---

## 9. Feature-Based Pricing
**Charge for specific AI capabilities**

### Examples:
- “AI scoring” add-on
- “Auto-summarization” module

### Why it works:
- Easy upsell within existing SaaS

### When to use:
- Adding AI into existing products (like ATS, CRM)

---

## How Top AI Companies Combine These

Most successful AI products **don’t use just one model**:

| Product Type     | Typical Pricing Stack                     |
|------------------|------------------------------------------|
| API companies    | Usage-based + volume discounts           |
| AI SaaS          | Subscription + usage caps                |
| Enterprise AI    | Seat-based + usage + custom              |
| AI marketplaces  | Credits + usage                          |

---

## Strategic Takeaways (PM Lens)

- **Cost structure matters** → LLM costs force usage pricing  
- **User psychology matters** → subscriptions feel safer  
- **Value clarity matters** → outcome pricing wins long-term  
- **Adoption matters** → freemium is key
